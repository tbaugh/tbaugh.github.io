Hello, world.

My name’s Travis and I’m an aspiring web developer. It feels a bit odd to say that after having already completed a degree and working in an industry for a few years, but I’m becoming more and more interested in the web each day and have decided to focus on developing skills in this exciting field.

About Me

I got my first taste of making websites and browsing the internet in about the 5th grade, however it wasn’t until recently, now well into my 20s, that I ever really knew more than how to mark up a blog post, upload via FTP, or create simple HTML pages with a little CSS and some cut/paste Javascript.

Since the beginning of the year I’ve been slowy working my way through The Odin Project, an open-source online bootcamp focusing on full-stack web development with Ruby on Rails. It's been a challenge working through the curriculum while maintaining a full-time job, social life, and other hobbies, but it is quite rewarding.

The Purpose of This Blog

I started this blog for a few reasons. First, I have the day off today and wanted to do something productive to start my day, I'd heard the name jekyll a few times before and thought I'd give it a shot. Most importatnly I wanted a space to record my educational journey or store all the tid-bits of inspiration I find along the way. If anything this will just be a good place to look back on if I get confused about how I executed some project later.

But if I'm learning Rails..?

I decided on jekyll vs. Rails for this blog in part because I've already made a blog in Rails as part of The Odin Project and there is something to be said for learning new things. But also jekyll seems perfect for my intentions. Rails just seems like overkill for a small personal site where I just want to throw up some HTML.

Conclusion

So until I have something more interesting to talk about, this post will be a testing area as I learn more.
